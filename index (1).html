Mission

My work focuses on a practical question: how can individual trading signals be combined into portfolio systems that remain more robust under friction, lag, changing regimes, and live validation?

The goal is not to present a magic trading model. The goal is to build a serious applied math and quantitative finance research portfolio that treats uncertainty, overfitting, and execution realism as first-class problems.

White Pony Research Themes
  • Portfolio Construction: combining multiple signals instead of relying on one standalone model.
  • Robustness Testing: checking whether results survive cost, lag, stress, and subperiod tests.
  • Execution Realism: treating fills, slippage, and rebalance timing as model-selection constraints.
  • Live Validation: comparing backtest behavior against paper/live deployment behavior.

Validation Philosophy

A model is not credible because it has an attractive historical chart. It becomes more credible when assumptions are documented, failure modes are tested, and live behavior is compared against the backtest without overclaiming short-term results.
